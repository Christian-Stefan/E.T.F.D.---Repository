How to approach a carbon-neutral future through the 4TU Lens?

  • 4TU.Energy will connect and build upon the individual strengths of the 4TUs and facilitate coordination and collaboration among researchers in the field of energy to accelerate the transition toward a carbon-neutral future.
  • A "Research Map" best illustrates the many disciplines, fields, and applications that collectively form the 4TU.Energy community. This Research Map showcases the majority of research topics related to Sustainable Energy Technology:
  • The Curriculum Overview highlights the major courses on both master level and bachelor level related to Sustainable Energy Technology, offered by Delft University of Technology, Eindhoven University of Technology, University of Twente and Wageningen University & Research. Each university's curriculum has its own strengths to address various aspects of energy studies, including sustainable energy technologies and environmental impact assessment.
 

How to read the Curriculum Overview? 

The hexagons with the darkest color and orange letters indicate the main categories in our selection. These categories include:

🔋Batteries

🖥Computer Systems

🧪Chemistry for Energy

🏭Industry

🧩System Transition

🌱Renewable Energy

🌡️Heat

⚛Hydrogen

🌐 Built Environment and Earth Systems

Courses are named individually. Courses for bachelor students are indicated as 'BSc' and courses for master students are indicated as 'MSc’. The info of "course offered by which university" can be distinguished by the text color:

  • Blue-TUDelft;
  • Red-TU/e;
  • Black-UTwente;
  • Green-WUR. The colors are aligned with the logo's color of each university.

Course details are either embedded in hyperlinks (seen as underlines); or for a few courses which weblinks for course details are not open to the public, course details are provided in PDF attachments (in a number sequence) which can be found in ‘Attachment pane’ in your PDF reader.
